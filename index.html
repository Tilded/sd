Combat Flow

1. Combat Setup

  1. Surprise Check (if applicable)
    • Hidden creature rolls DEX check
    • If beats passive detection ‚Üí Free turn before initiativeThe order in which characters act in combat
    • Free Turn: One action + move, attacks have advantageRoll 2d20, take higher, no reactions allowed
  2. Initiative: d20 + DEX (highest first)

2. Turn Structure

Each turn: Check conditions ‚Üí Choose action + movement ‚Üí Resolve

Movement Options Description
Action + Move One action + move "near" (~30 ft), can split
Double Move Forgo action, move "near" twice (~60 ft)

3. Combat Actions

Attack (Melee/Ranged)
  • Roll: d20 + STR/DEX + bonuses vs AC
  • Nat 20: Auto-hit, double damage dice
  • Nat 1: Auto-miss, possible consequence
  • Damage: Weapon dice + STR/DEX + bonuses
Spellcasting
  • Roll: d20 + INT/WIS vs Spell DC (10 + tier)
  • Nat 20: Auto-success, double one numerical effect
  • Nat 1: Fail + mishap, spell unavailable until rest
Other:

Use item, help ally, improvise (GM sets DC)

4. End of Round

  1. Dying Check: 0 HPHit Points - when reduced to 0, you become unconscious and dying = unconscious, dies next turn unless healed
  2. Monster Morale: Half HP ‚Üí DC 15 WIS or flee/surrender
  3. Light Timer: Real-time countdown continues
  4. Return to initiative order

Stealth & Detection

Hiding/Sneaking

  • When: Risky stealth situations
  • Roll: d20 + DEX vs DC (typically 12-15)
  • Modifiers:
    • Total darkness: Easier/automatic if unobserved
    • Cover/shadows: AdvantageRoll 2d20, take higher
    • Bright light: Harder/impossible
    • Noisy environment: Higher DC

Detection

  • Active Search: Describe searching specific area ‚Üí Auto-find if correct spot
  • Passive Detection: d20 + WIS vs DC (when not actively searching)
  • Modifiers:
    • Total darkness: DisadvantageRoll 2d20, take lower or impossible
    • Moving/noisy target: AdvantageRoll 2d20, take higher
    • Distracted searcher: DisadvantageRoll 2d20, take lower

Experience & Treasure

XP System

Characters gain XP from valuable treasures and boons earned during adventures. Each PC gets the full XP value of each treasure. When PCs level up, XP resets to zero.

Quality XP Examples
Poor 0 Bag of silver, used dagger, knucklebone dice
Normal 1 Bag of gold, gem, fine armor, magic scroll
Fabulous 3 Magic sword, giant diamond, mithral chainmail
Legendary 10 The Staff of Ord, djinni's wish, dragon hoard

XP Sources

  • Gold and gems
  • Oaths, secrets, and blessings
  • Magic items
  • Meaningful trophies/tokens
  • Clever thinking (award 1 XP for ingenious actions)

Treasure Value Guidelines

Per treasure find, each group should gain about 10 gp √ó average party level in value:

Levels 0-3 ~20 gp in value
Levels 4-6 ~50 gp in value
Levels 7-9 ~80 gp in value

Note: XP awards don't need to reflect monetary value - boons and fabled items have intangible worth beyond gold.

Resting & Recovery

Rest Requirements

  • Duration: 8 hours sleep + consume 1 ration
  • Interruptions: Each stressful event (combat, etc.) = DC 12 CON check
    • Failure: Consume ration, gain no benefit
    • Success: Full HP recovery + all stat damage healed + abilities refresh

Campfire Rules

  • Create: Combine 3 torches (can't move once lit)
  • Duration: 8 hours with character present
  • Range: Near distance light

Danger Level During Rest

Environment Encounter Check Frequency
Unsafe Every 3 hours
Risky Every 2 hours
Deadly Every hour

Exploration & Solo Play

Random Encounters

Purpose: Maintain time pressure - lingering = more risk

Check Frequency:

  • Dungeon Crawling: Every 3/2/1 rounds (Unsafe/Risky/Deadly)
  • Overland Travel: Every 3/2/1 hours (Unsafe/Risky/Deadly)
  • Trigger: Roll 1d6, encounter on 1 (also check for loud disturbances)

Encounter Setup:

  • Starting Distance: Roll 1d6 ‚Üí 1=Close, 2-4=Near, 5-6=Far
  • Activity: Roll 2d6 ‚Üí 2-4=Hunting, 5-6=Eating, 7-8=Building, 9-10=Socializing, 11=Guarding, 12=Sleeping
  • Reaction: Roll 2d6 + CHA mod ‚Üí 0-6=Hostile, 7-8=Suspicious, 9=Neutral, 10-11=Curious, 12+=Friendly
  • Treasure: 50% chance of no treasure

SoloDark Oracle System

  • Yes/No Questions: d20 oracle roll
  • Story Prompts: d100 for Verb + Noun combinations
  • Use frequently for solo narrative generation

Critical Rules

Light & Darkness

  • Total Darkness: DisadvantageRoll 2d20, take lower on most tasks
  • Dark-adapted monsters: Attack normally in darkness
  • Light sources: Tick down in real-time during play

Dying & Stabilization

  • 0 HPHit Points - your life force and ability to continue fighting: Unconscious and dying
  • Stabilize: Action + DC 15 INT check by ally
  • Death: Occurs at start of dying character's next turn

Special Situations

  • Monster abilities: May require saving throws
  • Player creativity: GM should reward clever descriptions over dice rolls
  • GM discretion: Fast and fun over strict rule adherence
Remember: Shadowdark emphasizes quick resolution, narrative flow, and the constant tension of dwindling resources. When in doubt, make a ruling that keeps the game moving and the players engaged with the dangers lurking in the shadows.
